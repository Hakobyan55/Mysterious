INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT ARMENIA

1.

Center of ancient civilization. Armenia, along with Iran, China, Greece, Egypt and Japan, is one of the six ancient countries in the world that have existed for thousands of years. Armenia was first mentioned in the Behistun Inscription of Darius I in 520 BC.

2.

Armenia is the first country to adopt Christianity as its state religion. The apostles Thaddeus and Bartholomew preached in Armenia and the Armenian Apostolic Church is named after them. Gregory the Illuminator, who baptized Armenia in 301, became the first Catholicos of All Armenians. Armenia became the first Christian state.

3.

The Armenian alphabet was created by Mesrop Mashtots in 405-406. The Armenian alphabet is considered one of the three most perfect in the world, along with the Georgian and Korean. Mashtots also created the Georgian and Albanian alphabets. His monument is located in the village of Artashavan, on the slopes of Mount Aragats.

4.

The Armenian Genocide was organized and carried out by the Ottoman Empire in 1915-1923, with mass massacres and deportations, with the aim of completely exterminating the Armenians. About 1.5 million Armenians were killed. The genocide was officially recognized and condemned by 26 countries.

5.

The Duduk is a UNESCO treasure. The music of the Armenian duduk is included in the UNESCO list of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. Armenia is considered the homeland of apricots. Apricots were known in Rome as early as the 1st century BC and were called “Armenian apple” (Latin Mela armeniaca). Arab geographer Ibn al-Faqih in his “Book of Countries” (903 AD) calls apricots “the fruit of Armenia”. The Armenian duduk is made from apricot wood.

6.

The oldest shoe in the world. In the same Areni cave, a 5,500-year-old leather shoe was found, completely preserved, in September 2008. It is made of a single piece of leather, is size 37 and is worn on the right leg.

7.

Carpet weaving traditions. Armenian carpets are famous for their intricate patterns and are considered to be among the oldest carpet weaving samples in the world.
