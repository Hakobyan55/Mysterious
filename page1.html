LOCATION

Armenia is located in the South Caucasus, in the western part of Asia. It borders the following countries To the north: Georgia To the east: Azerbaijan To the south: Iran To the west: Turkey

WEATHER

The climate of the Republic of Armenia is mainly mountainous and strictly continental. It is characterized by dry and hot summers and cold and snowy winters. In summer, dry spells and warm winds are very common. •Winds are common, especially in mountainous areas. •Spring and autumn are short, but variable, with sudden temperature fluctuations. •Winter is often accompanied by heavy snowfall, especially in high-mountainous regions. •Dry spells and warm winds are very common in summer.

CULTURE

The culture of Armenia has a millennia-old history and is rich in national characteristics. It was formed under the influence of different civilizations, while maintaining its originality.

LITERATURE

- After the invention of the Armenian alphabet (405), a rich literature was created, from Mesrop Mashtots to Hovhannes Shiraz.

ARCHITEQTURE

- Medieval churches and monasteries decorated with khachkars are notable.

MUSIC

- National instruments (duduk, zurna) and Gusan songs are widespread. has rich and diverse genres: folk, classical, folk-concert, etc.

ART GALLERY

- Sculpture and painting developed especially in the 19th-20th centuries. Armenian cinema began in the 1920s with the creation of the first Armenian film. In the 1960s-70s, Sergey Parajanov gained great popularity, whose films, for example, “The Color of Pomegranate”, became classics. National festivals and traditions - Vardavar, Trndez, Easter are an integral part of Armenian culture. Armenian culture is an important part of spiritual values and national identity, being passed down from generation to generation. INTERESTING FACTS ABOUT ARMENIA 1. Center of ancient civilization. Armenia, along with Iran, China, Greece, Egypt and Japan, is one of the six ancient countries in the world that have existed for thousands of years. Armenia was first mentioned in the Behistun Inscription of Darius I in 520 BC. 2. Armenia is the first country to adopt Christianity as its state religion. The apostles Thaddeus and Bartholomew preached in Armenia and the Armenian Apostolic Church is named after them. Gregory the Illuminator, who baptized Armenia in 301, became the first Catholicos of All Armenians.
