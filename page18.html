This cave still keeps many of its secrets. ​Bear Cave, also known as Arjeri Karandzav, is one of Armenia’s most remarkable natural wonders, located in the Vayots Dzor Province, approximately 3–4 km from the village of Arpi. It stands as the longest and deepest cave in Armenia, extending about 3.5 km in length and reaching depths of up to 130 meters .​ Origins and Discovery The cave was discovered by hunters in 1978, who stumbled upon it while exploring the area. Inside, they found ancient bear bones, which led to the cave's name, "Bear Cave" . These findings provide a glimpse into the prehistoric fauna of the region.​ Geological Features Karst Formation: Bear Cave is a karst cave, formed through the dissolution of soluble rocks, primarily calcium carbonate, resulting in a complex network of passages and chambers .​ Stalactites and Stalagmites: The cave boasts numerous stalactite and stalagmite formations, creating a mesmerizing underground landscape .​ Temperature: The average temperature inside the cave is around 10.2°C, providing a cool environment year-round .​ Safety and Accessibility Bear Cave is known for its narrow corridors and complex layout, making navigation challenging. Some passages require crawling, and the risk of getting lost is significant.
